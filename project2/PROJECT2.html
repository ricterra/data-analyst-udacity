INVESTIGATE A DATASET¶

The following lines of code was dedicated to analyze the No-Show-Appointments data in order to assess and complete the project 2. I started by looking at the general traits of the data, then I performed the necessary wrangling and cleaning. At last, I plotted some graphs for better visualization and conclusions.

Some questions to be analyzed:¶

1. Which one has missed more medical appointments, man or woman?¶

2. Is there a relationship between no-show and social factors such as scholarship, hipertension, diabetes, alcoholism?¶

3. What's the impact of SMS received in no-show ratio?¶

4. Is there a specific day or month where people tend not to show up in appointments?¶

Answer 2

1. Firstly, we have to make a consideration about scholarship in which the value 0 is for people with least and value 1 the most. Analyzing the first plot we can conclude that most of the people describe in the data has a low or no scholarship. People with higher scholarship tend to miss less appointments compared to lower ones, respectively, no-show ratio [no-show = "yes"] of 25.2% for higher and 48.6% for lower.¶

2. In general people diagnosed with any of the diseases and/or illness in the dataframe such as: hipertension, diabetes and alcoholism tend to miss less appointments. In each case, we can see that no-show ratio [no-show = "yes"] for these groups is low: hipertension (30.4%), diabetes (24.3%) and alcoholism (21.9%). Maybe It indicates that people that are aware of his/her illness are more careful about his/her own health and go to the doctor more frequently.¶

3. Handcap data is strongly based on one variable [handicap=0], so it's risky concludes that people with no disabilities or slightly move impairment are less likely to miss appointments.¶

Final Remarks

1. The no-show-appointment dataframe used in this study was near-mint and haven't required neither wrangling nor cleaning efforts with just 1 unexpected negative age value that could be easily removed. Most values were fine, no missing and no null values were found. In onder to analyze social aspects it was very clear that 1 values was set for YES and 0 values was set for NO, no greater explanation was given about grades between 0 and 1. Schedules and appointments dates were in merged into 1 object and so required further splitting into year, month and day, no more than that.¶

2. In general people diagnosed with any of the diseases and/or illness in the dataframe such as: hipertension, diabetes and alcoholism tend to miss less appointments. In each case, we can see that no-show ratio [no-show = "yes"] for these groups is low: hipertension (30.4%), diabetes (24.3%) and alcoholism (21.9%).
